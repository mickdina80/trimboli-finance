Privacy policy

Effective date: 3 March 2026

Trimboli Finance ("Trimboli Finance", "we", "us", "our") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

This privacy policy explains how we collect, use, disclose and protect your personal information.

1. What personal information we collect

We may collect personal information including your name, address, contact details, date of birth, financial information such as income, assets, liabilities and expenses, employment details, and other information relevant to providing our credit assistance services.

2. How we collect your information

We collect information directly from you when you contact us, complete a form, or engage our services. We may also collect information from third parties such as lenders, credit reporting bodies, and other service providers with your consent.

3. How we use your information

We use your personal information to provide mortgage broking services, assess your credit needs, identify suitable loan products, prepare and lodge applications, communicate with you, comply with our legal and regulatory obligations, and improve our services.

4. Disclosure of information

We may share your information with lenders and credit providers, aggregators and licensees, credit reporting bodies, legal and financial advisers, and regulators as required by law. We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

5. Security

We take reasonable steps to protect your personal information from misuse, interference, loss, unauthorised access, modification or disclosure. We use secure systems and limit access to authorised personnel only.

6. Access and correction

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you and to ask us to correct any inaccuracies. Please contact us at rocky@trimbolifinance.com.au to make a request.

7. Complaints

If you have a complaint about how we handle your personal information, please contact us first. If unsatisfied, you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at www.oaic.gov.au.
